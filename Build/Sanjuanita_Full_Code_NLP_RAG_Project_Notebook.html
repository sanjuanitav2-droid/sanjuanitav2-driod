Problem Statement¶

Business Context¶

The healthcare industry is rapidly evolving, with professionals facing increasing challenges in managing vast volumes of medical data while delivering accurate and timely diagnoses. The need for quick access to comprehensive, reliable, and up-to-date medical knowledge is critical for improving patient outcomes and ensuring informed decision-making in a fast-paced environment.

Healthcare professionals often encounter information overload, struggling to sift through extensive research and data to create accurate diagnoses and treatment plans. This challenge is amplified by the need for efficiency, particularly in emergencies, where time-sensitive decisions are vital. Furthermore, access to trusted, current medical information from renowned manuals and research papers is essential for maintaining high standards of care.

To address these challenges, healthcare centers can focus on integrating systems that streamline access to medical knowledge, provide tools to support quick decision-making, and enhance efficiency. Leveraging centralized knowledge platforms and ensuring healthcare providers have continuous access to reliable resources can significantly improve patient care and operational effectiveness.

Common Questions to Answer

1. Diagnostic Assistance: "What are the common symptoms and treatments for pulmonary embolism?"

2. Drug Information: "Can you provide the trade names of medications used for treating hypertension?"

3. Treatment Plans: "What are the first-line options and alternatives for managing rheumatoid arthritis?"

4. Specialty Knowledge: "What are the diagnostic steps for suspected endocrine disorders?"

5. Critical Care Protocols: "What is the protocol for managing sepsis in a critical care unit?"

Objective¶

As an AI specialist, your task is to develop a RAG-based AI solution using renowned medical manuals to address healthcare challenges. The objective is to understand issues like information overload, apply AI techniques to streamline decision-making, analyze its impact on diagnostics and patient outcomes, evaluate its potential to standardize care practices, and create a functional prototype demonstrating its feasibility and effectiveness.

Data Description¶

The Merck Manuals are medical references published by the American pharmaceutical company Merck & Co., that cover a wide range of medical topics, including disorders, tests, diagnoses, and drugs. The manuals have been published since 1899, when Merck & Co. was still a subsidiary of the German company Merck.

The manual is provided as a PDF with over 4,000 pages divided into 23 sections.

Query 1: What is the protocol for managing sepsis in a critical care unit?¶

  • Give IV fluids to help stabilize the patients condition

  • Start antibiotics early and adjust them once test results are available

  • Remove or drain the source of infection if needed

  • Continuously monitor the patients vital signs and key lab values in the ICU

  • Use medications to raise blood pressure if fluids alone are not enough

  • Provide oxygen support, including a ventilator if breathing becomes difficult

  • Offer general supportive care, such as managing blood sugar and using steroids when required

Query 2: What are the common symptoms for appendicitis, and can it be cured via medicine? If not, what surgical procedure should be followed to treat it?¶

  • Pain usually starts near the stomach or belly button and later moves to the lower right side

  • Common symptoms include nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, and mild fever

  • Pain often gets worse when moving, coughing, or being touched.

  • Appendicitis usually cannot be cured with medicine alone

  • The standard treatment is surgery to remove the appendix

  • Antibiotics are often given before or after surgery to prevent infection

Query 3: What are the effective treatments or solutions for addressing sudden patchy hair loss, commonly seen as localized bald spots on the scalp, and what could be the possible causes behind it?¶

  • Sudden hair loss in small patches is commonly called alopecia areata

  • It may happen due to the bodys immune system attacking hair follicles

  • Treatments can include steroid medicines, topical creams or hair-growth products

  • In some cases hair may grow back on its own over time

  • Stress or genetics may increase the chance of this condition

Query 4: What treatments are recommended for a person who has sustained a physical injury to brain tissue, resulting in temporary or permanent impairment of brain function?¶

  • The first priority is making sure the person can breathe and has stable blood pressure.

  • Oxygen is provided to protect the brain

  • Surgery may be needed in serious cases to reduce pressure on the brain.

  • Doctors closely monitor the patient to prevent further damage

  • Rehabilitation therapy is often required for recovery after the injury

Query 5: What are the necessary precautions and treatment steps for a person who has fractured their leg during a hiking trip, and what should be considered for their care and recovery?¶

  • Keep the injured leg still using a splint or support
  • Apply ice and raise the leg to reduce pain and swelling
  • Seek medical care to check how serious the fracture is
  • Treatment may involve a cast, splint, or surgery depending on severity
  • Follow care instructions during recovery to ensure proper healing
  • Avoid keeping the leg immobile for too long to prevent stiffness and weakness

def generate_ground_relevance_response(user_input, k=3, max_tokens=1024, temperature=0, top_p=0.95, top_k=50):
    global qna_system_message, qna_user_message_template
    global groundedness_rater_system_message, relevance_rater_system_message, user_message_template

    # This is a the new api that langchain has , I was getting an error.
    relevant_document_chunks = retriever.invoke(user_input)
    relevant_document_chunks = relevant_document_chunks[:k]

    context_list = [d.page_content for d in relevant_document_chunks]
    context_for_query = ". ".join(context_list)


    prompt = f"""[INST]{qna_system_message}
user: {qna_user_message_template.format(context=context_for_query, question=user_input)}
[/INST]"""

    response = llm(
        prompt,
        max_tokens=max_tokens,
        temperature=temperature,
        top_p=top_p,
        top_k=top_k
    )

    answer = response["choices"][0]["text"]

    # Groundedness prompt
    groundedness_prompt = f"""[INST]{groundedness_rater_system_message}
user: {user_message_template.format(context=context_for_query, question=user_input, answer=answer)}
[/INST]"""

    # Relevance prompt
    relevance_prompt = f"""[INST]{relevance_rater_system_message}
user: {user_message_template.format(context=context_for_query, question=user_input, answer=answer)}
[/INST]"""

    response_1 = llm(
        groundedness_prompt,
        max_tokens=max_tokens,
        temperature=temperature,
        top_p=top_p,
        top_k=top_k
    )

    response_2 = llm(
        relevance_prompt,
        max_tokens=max_tokens,
        temperature=temperature,
        top_p=top_p,
        top_k=top_k
    )

    return response_1["choices"][0]["text"], response_2["choices"][0]["text"]
